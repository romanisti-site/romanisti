# Content Guide

## Observation

An observation is the beginning of editorial work. It may arise from a current event, institutional memory, a prior match, a public statement, a place, an image, or a detail that deserves context.

Each observation requires:

- a durable title
- a concise description
- a publication date
- an author
- a publication state
- a language
- zero or more topics

## Status

- `draft` — retained in the repository and excluded from production
- `published` — included in lists, routes, feeds, and the sitemap

## Images

Editorial imagery should belong to Rome first. Plates remain portrait-oriented, restrained, contemplative, and free of people unless the constitutional source explicitly provides otherwise.
