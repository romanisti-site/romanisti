# ARS SCRIBENDI

## The Art of Writing

**Version 1.0 — Constitutionally Locked**

### Classification

**Editorial Formation**

### Constitutional Purpose

Romanisti does not seek a uniform style.

It seeks a recognizable voice.

Style can be imitated.

Voice must be cultivated.

The purpose of this document is not to prescribe sentences, but to preserve an editorial character that future generations of Romanisti may inherit.

Every editor will write differently.

Every Romanisti article should nevertheless feel as though it belongs to the same institution.

This document therefore concerns not grammar, but judgment.

Not technique, but restraint.

Not personality, but stewardship.

Writing is never an act of self-expression alone.

It is an act of institutional continuity.

---

## Constitutional Architecture

### I — Voice

How Romanisti speaks.

---

### II — Rhythm

How Romanisti moves.

Sentence length.

Silence.

Cadence.

Paragraph construction.

---

### III — Questions

How Romanisti asks.

Questions as invitations.

Never manipulation.

Never performance.

---

### IV — Quotations

How Romanisti uses the words of others.

Never decoration.

Always illumination.

---

### V — Introductions

How Romanisti begins.

Begin with observation.

Never begin by announcing conclusions.

Invite.

Do not persuade.

---

### VI — Development

How understanding unfolds.

Observation.

Context.

History.

Reflection.

Discovery.

---

### VII — Conclusions

How Romanisti ends.

Never summarize.

Leave room for contemplation.

The final sentence should continue after the page has ended.

---

### VIII — Restraint

Luxury through restraint.

Say less.

Trust the reader.

Avoid certainty where certainty is not possible.

---

### IX — Stewardship

The voice belongs to Romanisti.

Not the individual editor.

---
